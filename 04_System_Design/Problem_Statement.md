# Problem Statement

## Background

Mrs. B operates a small-to-medium scale poultry farm where eggs are currently graded manually according to their weight before packaging and sale.

The grading process depends entirely on human labour, requiring multiple workers to inspect, weigh, sort, and package eggs. While this approach has been effective in the past, increasing labour costs, inconsistent worker productivity, and frequent egg breakage have reduced the efficiency of the operation.

---

## Current Situation

The farm grades approximately **350 eggs per day**.

Manual grading and packing of a single crate containing **30 eggs** takes approximately **11 minutes**.

The grading process currently involves **10 workers**, and productivity varies depending on the workers' level of concentration and urgency.

Eggs are also frequently cracked during repeated manual handling, resulting in product loss and reduced profitability.

---

## Problem

The existing manual grading process:

- requires excessive human labour
- is time-consuming
- causes avoidable egg breakage
- depends heavily on worker consistency
- limits productivity
- increases operational costs

As production grows, these challenges become increasingly difficult to manage using manual methods alone.

---

## Engineering Need

The farm requires an affordable, reliable, and easy-to-operate egg grading system capable of automating the grading process while maintaining accurate weight classification and minimizing egg damage.

The solution should be suitable for small and medium-scale poultry farms where industrial egg grading systems are financially impractical.

---

## Desired Outcome

Develop a semi-automated egg grading machine capable of:

- transporting eggs safely
- accurately measuring egg weight
- automatically sorting eggs by weight category
- reducing manual labour
- minimizing egg breakage
- improving grading consistency
- increasing operational efficiency
