# Egg Carrier Design Study

## Project

Egg Grading Machine (EGM-01)

---

# Purpose

This study evaluates different egg carrier concepts for transporting eggs through the grading machine while minimizing breakage and ensuring consistent positioning during weighing and sorting.

---

# Design Requirements

The carrier shall:

- Hold one egg.
- Prevent rolling.
- Minimize vibration.
- Support eggs weighing 50–75 g.
- Allow automatic release.
- Be easy to manufacture.
- Be replaceable.
- Be durable.
- Be easy to clean.

---

# Design Alternatives

## Option 1 – U-Shaped Cradle

Description

A curved cradle supports the egg along its sides.

Advantages

- Simple.
- Easy to fabricate.
- Good support.

Disadvantages

- Egg may rotate slightly.

---

## Option 2 – V-Shaped Cradle

Description

Two angled surfaces support the egg.

Advantages

- Centers the egg automatically.
- Prevents rolling.
- Easy to manufacture.

Disadvantages

- Smaller contact area.
- Higher contact pressure.

---

## Option 3 – Silicone Cup

Description

Soft flexible cup.

Advantages

- Excellent protection.
- Absorbs vibration.
- Reduces breakage.

Disadvantages

- Higher cost.
- More difficult to manufacture.
- Harder to clean.

---

## Option 4 – 3D Printed Egg Pocket

Description

Custom egg-shaped holder.

Advantages

- Excellent positioning.
- Easy to customize.
- Lightweight.

Disadvantages

- Longer print time.
- Material strength depends on filament.

# Engineering Analysis

The carrier must securely support an egg while allowing repeatable positioning at the weighing station.

The design should minimize vibration and impact while remaining inexpensive and easy to manufacture.

A rigid carrier with smooth rounded contact surfaces is preferable to a completely enclosed holder because it simplifies egg release and cleaning.

---

# Final Engineering Decision

## Selected Option

To be determined after prototype evaluation.

## Preferred Concept

Modified U-shaped cradle with soft silicone or rubber contact pads.

## Reason

The modified U-shaped cradle combines good egg support, ease of fabrication, and reliable positioning while reducing the risk of shell damage through compliant contact surfaces.

## Advantages

- Low manufacturing cost.
- Easy replacement.
- Good positioning.
- Reduced breakage risk.
- Easy cleaning.

## Disadvantages

- Requires optimization of cradle geometry.
- Soft pads may wear over time.

## Future Considerations

- Injection-molded carrier.
- Food-grade silicone insert.
- Adjustable carrier sizes.
